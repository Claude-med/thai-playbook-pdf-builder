# Research And Credit Rules

Use this when the playbook depends on a video, article, official documentation, current product behavior, or external facts.

## Source Layers

Keep these layers separate in planning files and source credit:

1. **Original source**: what the original creator/video/article says or demonstrates.
2. **External sources**: official docs, primary sources, credible references, or current information used to verify and extend the playbook.
3. **Claude Work TH application**: the method, worksheet, prompt pack, examples, and practical workflow created for readers.

Do not blur opinion, source facts, and added recommendations.

## Reader-Facing Labels

In the final PDF, use simple labels only when they help:

- `From the source`: source idea or demonstration.
- `Useful extra context`: verified external context.
- `Use this`: Claude Work TH practical adaptation.
- `Needs verification`: plausible but not verified enough to publish as fact.

Avoid making the main PDF feel like an internal research report.

## When To Research

Research or verify when the topic involves:

- current AI tools or model behavior
- product features, pricing, APIs, or release status
- legal/compliance/platform rules
- public statistics or market claims
- safety limitations
- workflows where best practices change over time

Prefer official documentation and primary sources. Use reputable secondary sources only when primary sources are unavailable or too narrow.

## Research Notes Format

Use this structure:

```markdown
# Research Notes

## Source Material
- Title:
- Creator:
- URL:
- Access date:
- Used for:

## External Sources
| Source | URL | Access Date | Used For | Confidence |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| [name] | [url] | [date] | verification/background/recommendation | high/medium/low |

## Claims To Verify Later
- [claim] - why it needs checking
```

## Source Credit Format

Use this structure:

```markdown
# Source Credit

## YouTube / Original Source
- Title:
- Creator / Channel:
- URL:
- Publish date:
- Access date:

## External Sources
- [Source name], [URL], accessed [date].

## Claude Work TH Application
This playbook reorganizes and adapts the source material into a practical method, worksheet, prompt pack, worked example, and action plan for Thai readers. It is not a verbatim transcript or a replacement for the original source.
```

## Copyright And Visual Rules

- Do not copy long transcript passages.
- Do not paste screenshots, thumbnails, logos, or creator-owned visuals unless the user confirms rights.
- Short quotes are allowed only when needed and should be minimal.
- Redraw source ideas as original diagrams, mockups, tables, or worksheets.
- Credit the original source visibly.

## Claim Labels

Use clear internal labels in notes:

- `Original source`: directly from source material.
- `External source`: from external research.
- `Claude Work TH application`: created/adapted for practical use.
- `Needs verification`: plausible but not verified enough to publish as fact.
